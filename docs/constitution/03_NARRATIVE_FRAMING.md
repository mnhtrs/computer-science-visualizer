# Constitution Document 03: Narrative Framing

**Title:** Narrative Framing
**Purpose:** Establishes the timeless, project-wide laws governing how knowledge is introduced, framed, and communicated so that learners construct correct mental models without distortion.
**Status:** Frozen
**Version:** 3.0.0
**Dependencies:**

# 1. Purpose
Narrative exists solely to organize learning. Its purpose is not entertainment, drama, or fictional storytelling. Every narrative decision shall improve understanding of reality rather than increase engagement alone.

# 2. Reality Before Story
Reality shall never be modified to create a better story. Facts, mechanisms, and causal relationships always take precedence over narrative convenience. A learner must never remember an entertaining falsehood instead of a truthful mechanism.

# 3. Observation Before Explanation
A learner shall first observe a phenomenon before receiving an explanation. Explanation exists to organize observed reality rather than replace observation. Narration shall never reveal conclusions before sufficient evidence has been presented.

# 4. Question Before Answer
Learning begins by establishing a meaningful question. Every explanation shall answer a previously established uncertainty. Information shall never be introduced without a clear educational purpose.

# 5. Progressive Understanding
Knowledge shall be introduced in the smallest sequence that preserves causal understanding. Each step must depend only on knowledge already established. A learner shall never be required to accept unexplained assumptions.

# 6. Truthful Analogy
Analogies may be used only when they preserve the underlying structure of the represented system. Every structural relationship in an analogy must correspond to a real structural relationship in the target concept. When an analogy reaches its limit, that limit shall be made explicit.

# 7. Explicit Assumptions
Every explanation depends on assumptions. Assumptions shall be stated explicitly whenever they influence understanding. Learners must always be able to distinguish between observed fact and design decisions/conventions.

# 8. Cognitive Continuity
Every learning step shall connect directly to the learner's current mental model. Transitions between concepts must preserve continuity. Conceptual jumps that require invisible reasoning are prohibited.

# 9. Misconception Resolution
Instruction shall actively identify and resolve common misconceptions. Misconceptions shall never be ignored. The learner must be guided toward recognizing why a false intuition fails before introducing the correct model.

# 10. Neutral Presentation
Narration shall remain intellectually neutral. Language shall clarify rather than persuade. Emotional language, exaggeration, sensationalism, and rhetorical manipulation are prohibited.

# 11. Learner Autonomy
The learner is an active participant in constructing knowledge. Narration shall guide reasoning without replacing it. Whenever possible, learners shall predict outcomes and verify their own understanding before receiving confirmation.

# 12. Universality
These constitutional laws apply regardless of educational domain, language, culture, medium, or interaction style.

# 13. Knowledge Reveal Law
A technical term or concept may be introduced only after the learner has observed a phenomenon that makes that term necessary. Terminology shall never precede the experience that motivates it.

# 14. Narrative-Driven Explanation
Educational explanation must emerge from the current state of the Journey. A component shall never be described in isolation, independent of the narrative context in which it is encountered in the story.

# 15. Plain Language and Technical Lock
Technical IT terminology must be explained using everyday language accessible to absolute beginners. While the core names of components, architectures, and protocols must be locked to their global English origins (to prevent unhelpful localized translations that limit real-world utility), the surrounding explanations must be written in natural, clear native prose.
